Privacy by design
Three layers of data protection
Client-side processing only
Excel files are parsed entirely in the browser using SheetJS. Student data never leaves the teacher's device and is never transmitted to any external server. This eliminates a whole category of PDPA risk before it can arise.
Human-in-the-loop approval
AI generates parent letters, but a teacher must review and explicitly approve before anything is sent. An AI-generated letter sent without review is an institutional liability — this gate is non-negotiable.
Full audit trail
Every generated letter is logged with: who generated it, timestamp, which student, and send status. The Sent log tab provides a complete record for institutional accountability.
Production roadmap
n8n automation — from demo to deployment

PDPA compliance
Singapore Personal Data Protection Act
Purpose limitation
Student data is used solely for parent reporting. Not retained beyond the active session, not shared with third parties.
Transparency
AI-generated content is clearly labelled. Parents receive letters reviewed and approved by a named teacher — not anonymous AI output.
Access control
Subject teachers access only their own students' data. Form teachers see class-level data. Role separation built into the login system.
Accountability
Audit log records every action — who generated each report, when, and confirmation of teacher approval before send.
💡
Why governance is built in from the start — not added later
Most data systems treat governance as a compliance checkbox — something added after the system is built. This system inverts that: every feature was designed with accountability in mind. The browser-only parsing wasn't a technical constraint, it was a privacy choice. The teacher approval gate wasn't a UX decision, it was a governance requirement. The audit log wasn't an afterthought, it was the first thing designed. This reflects five years of understanding what happens when student data is mishandled in a school — and what it costs.
